Main Points

Most data science talks tell you how to do 2 group comparisons, i.e. "A/B" tests, with the t-test.

I'm here to share two messages.

Message 1: You can do comparisons beyond just group "A" and group "B". You can add groups "C", "D", "E" and more.

Message 2: Using a probabilistic programming language, such as PyMC3, lets you do this in a principled fashion with a wonderful API.

When I see a lonely number in a news report, it always triggers an alarm: What should this lonely number be compared to?
  • Comparison is a cornerstone activity of scientific inquiry.
  • Without comparison, how do we know if a change has an effect?
